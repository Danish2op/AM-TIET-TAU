# AM TIET TAU Website Design

## Goal

Build a first-draft Vercel-hosted website for the TIET-TAU Centre of Excellence in Advanced Manufacturing using the exact brochure wording and brochure-backed facts where available.

## Source Direction

- Use the brochure wording: "Centre of Excellence in Advanced Manufacturing".
- Use brochure-backed claims, including India's first InssTek Directed Energy Deposition MX-Fab3 5-axis system.
- Treat the DSAI site as a structural reference only. Borrow the centre-site section flow, not its visual volume.
- Use local photos as evidence: DED machine, Wire EDM machine, interior process view, fabricated metal component, and material samples.
- Keep personal email exposure conservative on the first draft: make `coeam@thapar.edu` the primary contact.

## First-Draft Structure

1. Hero with real DED equipment image, centre name, TIET-TAU collaboration, and direct CTAs.
2. Capability strip for DED, Wire EDM, materials characterization, post-processing, training, and consultancy.
3. About section with a concise, factual description.
4. Core infrastructure section with DED, Wire EDM, complementary labs, and finishing zones.
5. Research and application areas focused on additive manufacturing, materials, FGMs, multimaterials, repair, precision machining, and Industry 4.0.
6. Industry offerings for process development, prototyping, qualification, training, sponsored R&D, and consultancy.
7. Leadership cards for Prof. Noam Eliaz, Prof. Bhaskar Chandra Mohanty, and Dr. Vineet Srivastava.
8. Contact section with email and TIET Patiala address.

## Visual Direction

- Palette: deep navy, steel blue, technical blue, white, cool gray, and a small TIET red accent.
- Style: restrained academic-industrial, evidence-led, technical, and readable.
- Motifs: clean grids, subtle layer/toolpath linework, compact specs, real machinery photography.
- Avoid: generic blue-glow AI visuals, glassmorphism, carousels, fake dashboards, stock imagery, decorative orbs, and vague filler copy.

## Verification

- Build must pass.
- Content tests should verify the main brochure-backed claims.
- Responsive layout should be checked at mobile and desktop widths before deployment.
